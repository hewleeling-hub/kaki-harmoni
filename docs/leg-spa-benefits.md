# Leg / foot spa — benefits & differentiation

Reference for marketing copy. Source: **GrandSun SG-2000 Home Spa booklet** (the
hydrosonic spa machine used at Kaki Harmoni). Extracted for the website's
"why book" content.

## What the machine actually is
Not a bucket of hot water — a **hydrosonic (ultrasonic) spa** that combines:
ultrasonic micro-bubbles, ozone, negative ions, and far-infrared warmth, with
optional herbal bath oils.

## How it differs from a plain hot-water soak at home
- **Deep, penetrating warmth (the biggest difference).** Ultrasonic energy carries
  heat *into* the tissue (the body is ~66% water, which absorbs it), warming you
  through at a comfortable ~38°C in about 10–15 minutes. Plain hot water only warms
  the surface and cools quickly — the booklet notes water at 42°C would take ~90
  minutes to reach the same depth.
- **Ultrasonic bubble massage.** ~100,000 tiny bubbles per second gently massage the
  soles and pressure points (the sole is the body's "second heart"). Still water does
  nothing.
- **Ozone-sanitised water.** Ozone infusion keeps the soak hygienic and deodorises
  tired feet. Tap water at home doesn't.
- **Negative ions + far-infrared.** "Forest waterfall" freshness and a gentle deep
  heat that add to the relaxation and circulation effect.
- **Essential aromatherapy oils** (rosemary, lavender, eucalyptus, pine, chamomile,
  juniper, hayseed — seven in all) tailored to a mood — calm/sleep, sore muscles,
  refresh. Staff personalise the blend per customer.

## Benefits (framed for tired feet & legs — safe, defensible)
- Relaxes and relieves tension after a long day on your feet.
- Deep warmth eases stiffness and comforts achy legs.
- Supports circulation down to the feet.
- Gentle massage of the soles and pressure points.
- Cleanses and freshens feet hygienically.
- Calms the mind — a genuine 15-minute reset.

## ⚠️ Do NOT use these on the website
The booklet is a multi-level-marketing piece with **strong, unverified medical
claims** — curing strokes, shrinking tumours, reversing diabetes/arthritis,
fertility effects, disease-recovery testimonials, calorie-burn / "equal to a 3–5km
run" numbers. **Keep all of it off the site.** Health-cure claims for a foot spa are
misleading and a legal risk. Stick to relaxation, comfort, warmth, circulation, and
hygiene — that story is both compelling and safe.
